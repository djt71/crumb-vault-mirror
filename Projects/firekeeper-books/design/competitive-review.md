## Evaluation Framework

For each title, evaluate on your **actual Kindle device** (not just the app). Read at least one full chapter.

### Visual Quality
- Illustration style — what technique? AI-generated or human? Consistent across the book?
- Illustration count and placement — how many? Full-page or inline?
- Cover design — would this stop you in a search result?
- Does it look like "book illustration" or "AI art"?

### Reading Experience
- Typography — font choice, sizing, line spacing. Does it feel considered or default?
- Margins and whitespace — cramped or breathing?
- Chapter openers — designed or just bold text?
- Illustration placement relative to text — integrated or pasted-in?
- How do illustrations render on e-ink vs. tablet? Do they load cleanly?
- Does the chapter flow feel interrupted by illustrations or enhanced by them?

### Product Quality
- Does this feel like a product someone cared about, or a cash-grab on PD text?
- Would you recommend this to someone? Would you gift it?
- What's the gut reaction in 3 seconds of Look Inside preview? (That's what your buyer gets.)

### Series Identity (where applicable)
- Does it feel like part of a series? How?
- Would you buy another title from this publisher based on this edition?
- What structural elements create series consistency? (Color, typography, layout, ornaments)

### Price vs. Value
- At the listed price, did you feel like you got what you paid for?
- What price would you have been willing to pay?
- What specific element most justified (or failed to justify) the price?

### Competitive Gap
- What's missing from this edition that Firekeeper could provide?
- What did this edition do well that Firekeeper should learn from?
- On a product page next to this, would a Firekeeper edition clearly stand out?
